Definition

What is a Hybrid Intelligence Advisor?

A Hybrid Intelligence (HI) Advisor is a sophisticated computational partner that synergistically combines human expertise and intuition with AI's vast computational power and data processing capabilities.

It's not about replacing the expert; it's about augmenting their abilities to solve complex problems more effectively and creatively.

For Academia

HI Advisors for Clemson A+H Professors

1. Research Grant Proposal Advisor

An advisor that actively assists faculty in identifying funding opportunities and crafting compelling grant proposals. It streamlines the pre-award process by leveraging data-driven insights.

  • Performs targeted funding discovery by matching faculty research with specific grant calls.
  • Provides data-driven proposal optimization, suggesting structure and keywords based on successful past grants.

2. Interdisciplinary Curriculum Designer

A tool to help design innovative courses and curricula that bridge architecture, health, and other relevant fields, ensuring programs remain at the cutting edge.

  • Conducts automated curriculum mapping to identify knowledge gaps and overlaps across departments.
  • Aids in fostering interdisciplinary connections by suggesting relevant readings, case studies, and potential collaborators.
For Practice

HI Advisors for Practicing Architects

1. Evidence-Based Design (EBD) Advisor

This advisor empowers architects to integrate the latest health research directly into their design workflow, ensuring projects are optimized for occupant well-being.

  • Provides real-time access to a curated EBD research repository during the design phase.
  • Focuses on linking design decisions directly to measurable health outcomes, strengthening the value proposition for clients.

2. Healthy Building Material Advisor

Navigates the complex world of building materials to help architects make informed decisions based on health, sustainability, and lifecycle cost metrics.

  • Utilizes toxicology and lifecycle data analysis to evaluate materials beyond simple aesthetics or cost.
  • Generates optimized material specification sheets that align with project health goals and certifications like WELL or LEED.

Anticipated Questions

Mitigating Critical Challenges

How do we handle AI inaccuracy and ensure reliable content?

Reliability is paramount. Unlike general AI, HI Advisors mitigate inaccuracy through a structured approach:

  • Curation of High-Quality Datasets: Models are trained on vetted, domain-specific information (e.g., peer-reviewed journals, established design guidelines) rather than the entire web.
  • Expert Validation Loops: Human experts are integral to the system, constantly reviewing and correcting AI-generated content to refine the model's accuracy over time.
  • Transparent Sourcing: The advisor provides clear citations and links to the source material, allowing users to verify information and understand the basis of its recommendations.
Justification

  • Domain-Specific Expertise: HI Advisors are purpose-built with a deep, structured understanding of Architecture + Health, providing nuanced and context-aware guidance.
  • Curated & Reliable Data Sources: They operate on a foundation of trusted, vetted information, ensuring recommendations are accurate and defensibleâ€”a crucial factor for grants and building projects.
  • Proactive & Structured Guidance: Instead of waiting for a prompt, HI Advisors can guide users through complex workflows (e.g., the stages of grant writing), offering timely, structured advice.
  • Integration with Program-Specific Workflows: They can be tailored to connect with Clemson's unique resources, databases, and faculty expertise, creating a seamless and powerful ecosystem.

The contemporary discourse surrounding Artificial Intelligence (AI) in creative and professional domains is often characterized by a narrative of automation and replacement. However, this perspective overlooks a more sophisticated and potent paradigm: augmentation.


The true transformation lies not in creating autonomous agents that supplant human expertise, but in developing collaborative partners that amplify the architect's and researcher's innate creativity, judgment, and domain-specific knowledge. This presentation introduces a framework for a new class of professional tools known as Hybrid Intelligence (HI) Advisors.

Hybrid Intelligence is not merely a technical combination of software methods but a strategic and philosophical approach to AI development. It is defined as the intentional synthesis of human and machine intelligence, a partnership designed to expand human intellect and achieve goals that were previously unreachable by either entity working in isolation.


The foundational principle of HI is that AI systems should be developed as collaborators that augment human capabilities, not as autonomous systems that replace them. These systems are not opaque "black box" oracles but transparent, interactive partners that leverage AI's formidable analytical power while explicitly preserving and prioritizing human judgment, ethical reasoning, and creative agency.
